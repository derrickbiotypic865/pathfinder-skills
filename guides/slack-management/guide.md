# Take Back Your Slack — A Practical Guide to Slack Management and Organization

> New to technical terms? See the [Plain-English Glossary](../glossary.md) for jargon-free definitions.

## The Problem

You're in 147 channels. Your sidebar is a wall of bold text. Notifications ping you every 30 seconds. You know someone sent you that important link last week, but searching for it returns 400 results. You spend half your day in Slack and the other half recovering from being in Slack. Sound familiar?

This guide gives you a system to organize your Slack so it works for you instead of against you. No apps to install — just the features Slack already has that most people never set up.

## What You'll Learn

1. How to organize your sidebar so you see what matters
2. How to tame notifications so you're not interrupted constantly
3. How to use threads (and why everyone will thank you)
4. How to find anything with search
5. Keyboard shortcuts that save real time
6. Status and availability — tell people when you're focused
7. Huddles — quick calls without leaving Slack
8. A simple system for staying organized going forward

## Time Needed

About 30-40 minutes. You'll set things up as you go, so your Slack will be calmer by the time you're done.

## Prerequisites

- A Slack workspace you use regularly
- A willingness to mute some channels

**Mac users:** Wherever you see `Ctrl` in this guide, use `Cmd` instead. Everything else works the same.

## Part 1: Organize Your Sidebar

The sidebar is the first thing you see. If it's a mess, everything feels chaotic. Let's fix it.

### Star Your Important Channels

Starred channels appear at the top of your sidebar — always visible, easy to reach.

**Try it now:**
1. Right-click any channel in the sidebar
2. Click **Star channel**
3. It moves to the **Starred** section at the top

Star the 5-10 channels you actually check every day. Everything else can live further down.

**What to star:**
- Your team channel
- Your project channels (the active ones)
- Direct messages with your manager or key collaborators
- Any channel where you're expected to respond quickly

### Create Sidebar Sections (custom groups)

Sections let you group channels your way — like creating folders for your sidebar.

**Try it now:**
1. Hover over the sidebar and look for **Channels** heading
2. Click the **three dots** next to it (or right-click)
3. Click **Create a new section**
4. Name it (e.g., "My Team", "Projects", "Noise")
5. Drag channels into your new section

**Suggested sections:**

| Section | What goes in it |
|:--------|:----------------|
| **My Team** | Your team channel, manager DM, standup channel |
| **Active Projects** | Channels for things you're working on right now |
| **Reference** | Channels you read but rarely post in (announcements, company news) |
| **Low Priority** | Channels you check once a day or less |

**Collapse sections you don't need right now:** Click the section name to collapse it. All those channels are still there — they're just not cluttering your view.

### Mute Channels You Don't Need Constantly

Muting a channel means it won't show bold text or send notifications unless someone mentions you directly.

**Try it now:**
1. Right-click a channel you don't need to monitor constantly
2. Click **Mute channel**
3. The channel turns gray in your sidebar — still there, but quiet

**What to mute:**
- Large announcement channels (check once a day)
- Social/fun channels (check when you want a break)
- Channels you were added to but don't actively participate in
- Anything with more than 50 people (these are usually noisy)

**You can always unmute later.** Muting isn't leaving — you're still in the channel. You just don't get bothered by every message.

### Leave Channels You Don't Need

If you haven't read a channel in a month, leave it.

1. Open the channel
2. Click the channel name at the top
3. Scroll down and click **Leave channel**

You can always rejoin later by searching for it. But right now, it's just noise.

**The test:** For each channel, ask: "If I missed every message in here for a week, would it matter?" If no — mute or leave.

## Part 2: Tame Your Notifications

Slack's default notifications are designed to get your attention for EVERYTHING. That's why it feels overwhelming. Let's make it smarter.

### Set Your Global Notification Preferences

1. Click your **profile picture** in the top-right
2. Click **Preferences**
3. Click **Notifications**

**Recommended settings:**

- **Notify me about**: Change from "All new messages" to **Direct messages, mentions & keywords**. This is the single biggest change — you'll only get pinged when someone actually needs YOU.
- **Sound & appearance**: Turn off sounds if they distract you. The visual badge is enough.
- **When I'm not active**: Choose how mobile notifications work. Consider "Only send notifications for DMs and mentions."

### Set a Notification Schedule

You don't need Slack pinging you at 10 PM.

1. In Preferences > Notifications, find **Notification schedule**
2. Set your hours (e.g., 8 AM to 6 PM, weekdays only)
3. Outside these hours, Slack stays silent

### Set Channel-Specific Notifications

Some channels matter more than others. You can customize notifications per channel.

1. Open the channel
2. Click the channel name at the top
3. Click **Notifications** (or **Get notifications for...**)
4. Choose:
   - **Every new message** — for critical channels (your team, incident response)
   - **Mentions only** — for most channels (the default after you change global settings)
   - **Nothing** — for muted channels you browse manually

### Use Keywords

Get notified when specific words are mentioned, even in channels you've muted.

1. Go to Preferences > Notifications
2. Find **My keywords**
3. Add words you care about (e.g., your project name, your product, "deadline", "urgent")
4. Slack will notify you whenever these words appear in any channel you're in

## Part 3: Use Threads (and Why Everyone Will Thank You)

Threads are the single most important Slack feature for keeping things organized. If your team doesn't use threads, everything blends into one long scroll of confusion.

### What Threads Are

A thread is a side conversation attached to a specific message. Instead of responding in the main channel (where it gets lost in the flow), you respond in a thread (where it stays connected to the original topic).

### How to Use Threads

**Starting a thread:**
1. Hover over any message
2. Click the **speech bubble icon** (Reply in thread)
3. Type your response in the thread panel that opens on the right

**Reading a thread:**
- Click the **"X replies"** link below any message that has responses

### Why Threads Matter

**Without threads:**
```
Alice: Anyone know the status of the Q3 report?
Bob: I had pizza for lunch
Carol: @Alice it's almost done, reviewing now
Dave: Can someone help with the printer?
Alice: @Carol great, when can I expect it?
Bob: The new coffee machine is broken again
Carol: @Alice end of day today
```

Good luck following that conversation.

**With threads:**
The main channel stays clean. Alice's question and all responses live in one thread. Bob's pizza and Dave's printer are separate. You can follow the conversations you care about and ignore the ones you don't.

### Thread Etiquette

- **Reply in the thread, not the channel.** If someone started a thread, continue there.
- **"Also send to channel"** checkbox: Use this sparingly. Only check it when the whole channel needs to see your reply (like a final answer or important update).
- **Start a new thread for a new topic.** Don't hijack someone else's thread with a different question.

## Part 4: Find Anything with Search

Slack search is powerful but most people just type a word and hope for the best. Here's how to actually find things.

### Basic Search

Press **Ctrl+K** (or **Cmd+K** on Mac) or click the search bar at the top. Type what you're looking for.

### Search Modifiers (the real power)

These narrow your results dramatically:

| Modifier | What it does | Example |
|:---------|:-------------|:--------|
| `from:@name` | Messages from a specific person | `from:@alice budget` |
| `in:#channel` | Messages in a specific channel | `in:#marketing report` |
| `to:me` | Messages sent directly to you | `to:me deadline` |
| `has:link` | Messages that contain a URL | `has:link dashboard` |
| `has:file` | Messages with file attachments | `has:file presentation` |
| `before:2026-03-01` | Messages before a date | `before:2026-03-01 quarterly` |
| `after:2026-01-01` | Messages after a date | `after:2026-01-01 budget` |
| `during:March` | Messages during a month | `during:March standup notes` |
| `is:saved` | Messages you've bookmarked | `is:saved` |

**Combine them for precision:**
- `from:@alice in:#marketing has:file` — files Alice shared in the marketing channel
- `after:2026-03-01 in:#project-x deadline` — mentions of "deadline" in project-x since March

### Save Messages for Later

See something important? Bookmark it so you can find it instantly later.

1. Hover over the message
2. Click the **bookmark icon** (or the three dots > **Save message**)
3. Find all saved messages: click **Bookmarks** in the sidebar (or press **Ctrl+Shift+S**)

Use this for: action items, important links, decisions, anything you'll need to reference later.

### Pin Important Messages

Pins are like bookmarks but for the whole channel — everyone can see them.

1. Hover over the message
2. Click the three dots > **Pin to channel**
3. Anyone in the channel can click the **pin icon** at the top to see all pinned messages

Good things to pin: project links, meeting notes, team agreements, onboarding docs.

## Part 5: Keyboard Shortcuts

These are the shortcuts that actually save time in daily use. You don't need all of them — pick 2-3 to start.

### Navigation

| Shortcut | What it does |
|:---------|:-------------|
| `Ctrl+K` | **Quick switcher** — jump to any channel, DM, or person by typing their name. The single most useful Slack shortcut. |
| `Ctrl+Shift+K` | Open a new direct message |
| `Ctrl+Shift+A` | Show all unread messages |
| `Alt+Up/Down Arrow` | Move to the previous/next channel in sidebar |
| `Alt+Shift+Up/Down` | Move to the previous/next unread channel |
| `Esc` | Mark the current channel as read |

### Messages

| Shortcut | What it does |
|:---------|:-------------|
| `Up Arrow` | Edit your last message (press in the message box when it's empty) |
| `Shift+Enter` | New line without sending |
| `Ctrl+Shift+\` | Toggle the emoji picker |
| `Ctrl+B` | Bold selected text |
| `Ctrl+I` | Italic selected text |
| `Ctrl+Shift+C` | Code format selected text |
| `Ctrl+Shift+7` | Numbered list |
| `Ctrl+Shift+8` | Bulleted list |

### The Big Three (start with these)

| Shortcut | What it does |
|:---------|:-------------|
| `Ctrl+K` | Quick switcher — jump to any channel or person instantly |
| `Esc` | Mark current channel as read (clear the bold) |
| `Up Arrow` | Edit your last message (fix typos fast) |

**Try this now:** Press `Ctrl+K`, start typing the name of a channel or person, press Enter. You just navigated without touching your mouse or scrolling the sidebar.

## Part 6: Status and Availability

Your status tells people whether you're available, focused, or away. Using it well means fewer "are you there?" messages.

### Set Your Status

1. Click your **profile picture** in the top-right
2. Click **Update your status**
3. Pick a preset or type a custom status
4. Set a **clear after** time so you don't forget to remove it

**Useful statuses:**

| Status | When to use |
|:-------|:------------|
| **In a meeting** | During meetings — people will wait instead of pinging you |
| **Focusing** | When you need deep work time — sets expectations |
| **Out sick** | Self-explanatory |
| **Vacationing** | People will find someone else instead of waiting |
| **Lunch** | Quick — back soon |

### Do Not Disturb (Pause Notifications)

Need to focus for an hour? Pause notifications temporarily.

1. Click your **profile picture**
2. Click **Pause notifications**
3. Choose a duration: 30 minutes, 1 hour, 2 hours, or until tomorrow
4. Notifications stop. You'll see them when you unpause.

People can still send you messages — they'll just be waiting for you when you come back. If something is truly urgent, Slack gives them an option to notify you anyway.

**Keyboard shortcut:** `Ctrl+Shift+D` toggles Do Not Disturb.

## Part 7: Huddles — Quick Calls Without Leaving Slack

Huddles are lightweight audio calls that happen right inside a Slack channel or DM. Think of them as "tapping someone on the shoulder" — much faster than scheduling a meeting.

### Starting a Huddle

1. Open any channel or DM
2. Click the **headphones icon** in the bottom-left of the channel
3. You're live — other people in the channel can join by clicking the same icon

### When to Use Huddles

- **Instead of a long back-and-forth thread:** "This is getting complicated — let me just huddle with you for 2 minutes"
- **Quick questions:** Faster than typing, less formal than a calendar invite
- **Pair working:** Screen share while you work through something together
- **Impromptu standups:** "Hey team, quick huddle to align"

### Huddle Features

- **Screen share:** Click the screen icon during a huddle to share your screen
- **Reactions:** Click emoji reactions during a huddle (thumbs up, etc.) without unmuting
- **Invite people:** Click the people icon to invite others to join
- **Leave:** Click the headphones icon again or the hang up button

### Huddle Etiquette

- Huddles are **informal and optional.** Don't expect everyone to join immediately.
- If someone doesn't join, they might be focused. Send a message: "Hey, I started a huddle to talk about X — join when you can"
- Keep huddles short. If it's going to be more than 10 minutes, consider a proper meeting.

## Part 8: A Simple System for Staying Organized

Here are the habits that keep Slack manageable long-term. None of these take more than a few seconds.

### The Morning Scan (2 minutes)

When you open Slack each morning:
1. Press **Ctrl+Shift+A** to see all unread messages
2. Scan the list — handle quick replies immediately
3. For anything that needs more thought, **save the message** (bookmark icon) and come back later
4. Press **Esc** on channels that don't need a response to mark them as read

### The "Inbox Zero" Approach

Treat your Slack sidebar like an inbox:
- **Bold channels = unread = needs attention**
- When you've read a channel and don't need to act on it, press `Esc` to mark it as read
- Goal: Clear the bold. If everything is gray, you're caught up.

### Respond in Threads, Always

Every time you reply to something, reply in the thread — not in the main channel. This keeps channels readable for everyone.

### Save Instead of Scroll

When you see something you'll need later:
- **Bookmark it** (hover > bookmark icon)
- **Pin it** if the whole team needs it
- Don't rely on "I'll scroll back and find it" — you won't.

### Friday Cleanup (5 minutes)

Every Friday:
1. Look at your sidebar sections — are channels in the right groups?
2. Mute anything that's been noisy but not useful
3. Leave channels you haven't read in 2+ weeks
4. Review saved messages — act on them or clear them
5. Check your pinned messages — remove outdated pins

### Batch Your Slack Time

The biggest productivity killer is checking Slack constantly. Instead:
- **Check Slack 3-4 times a day** (morning, midday, afternoon, end of day)
- **Close Slack during focused work** — or use Do Not Disturb
- **Respond in batches** — not every message needs an instant reply
- **Set expectations:** If your team expects instant replies, have a conversation about response times. Most things can wait an hour.

## You're Done!

You now have a system for keeping Slack organized. The key changes:

1. **Notifications set to mentions only** — you won't be pinged for every message
2. **Sidebar organized** — starred channels on top, sections for grouping, noisy channels muted
3. **Threads** — keep channels clean by replying in threads
4. **Search** — find anything with modifiers instead of scrolling

Start with the Big Three shortcuts (`Ctrl+K` to jump anywhere, `Esc` to mark as read, `Up Arrow` to edit) and the notification change (mentions only). Those two moves alone will transform how Slack feels.

The goal isn't to be in Slack less — it's to be in Slack smarter. Check it on your terms, not every time it beeps.
